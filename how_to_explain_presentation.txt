SLIDE 3 — PROBLEM (Why did you build this app?)
----------------------------------------------------------------
"So the problem is this —

Pregnant women in rural Karnataka don't have access to
good health information. Most apps are only in English.
Many villages don't have internet.
The paper health cards they get from hospitals get lost easily.

So I built an app that:
- Works WITHOUT internet (completely offline)
- Is in BOTH Kannada and English
- Helps mothers track their pregnancy week by week"

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 4 — WHAT THE APP DOES (Simple features explanation)
----------------------------------------------------------------
"The app has 5 main features:

1. KICK COUNTER
   - The mother taps a button every time the baby kicks
   - If the baby kicks less than 10 times in 2 hours,
     the app sends a warning notification
   - This is important to detect if the baby is in danger

2. CHECKUP SCHEDULER
   - The mother can add upcoming doctor appointments
   - The app shows a countdown — like '4 days remaining'
   - It also sends a reminder notification before the checkup

3. NUTRITION TRACKER
   - It shows a checklist of 10 healthy foods
   - Like Ragi, Milk, Egg, Banana, Iron Tablet etc.
   - The mother checks off what she ate that day
   - It resets automatically every morning

4. HEALTH ALERTS
   - If the mother has danger signs like
     severe headache, bleeding, or baby not moving —
     she selects those signs in the app
   - The app immediately shows a big red screen saying
     'GO TO HOSPITAL IMMEDIATELY'
   - There is a button to call 104 (free helpline) directly

5. WEEKLY MILESTONES (This is the AI part)
   - Every week, the app shows what is happening with the baby
   - Like — 'Week 8: Baby is the size of a kidney bean. Heart is beating!'
   - This text is in BOTH Kannada and English
   - All 33 weeks of content (week 8 to week 40) were
     GENERATED using Google Gemini AI"

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 7 — ARCHITECTURE (If they ask about MVVM)
----------------------------------------------------------------
"The app follows MVVM architecture.
This is a popular design pattern used in professional apps.

Think of it in 3 layers:

1. The SCREEN (what the user sees) — called the View
2. The BRAIN (the logic) — called the ViewModel
3. The DATABASE (where data is saved) — called the Model

The screen never talks directly to the database.
It always goes through the ViewModel.
This makes the code clean, testable, and easy to maintain."

----------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 8 — BACKGROUND WORKERS (If they ask about notifications)
----------------------------------------------------------------
"The app sends 3 types of automatic notifications:

1. Every 12 hours — reminds the mother about her upcoming checkup
2. Every 2 hours — reminds her to track the baby's kicks
3. Every midnight — resets the food checklist for the new day

These notifications work even when the app is closed.
I used something called WorkManager to do this.
It also works after the phone is restarted."

COMMON QUESTIONS THEY MIGHT ASK — SIMPLE ANSWERS
================================================================

Q: What is MVVM?
A: It is a design pattern. It separates the screen, the logic,
   and the database into 3 layers so the code is clean and organised.

Q: What database did you use?
A: Room Database. It is a local database that saves data
   directly on the phone. No server or internet needed.

Q: How did you use AI in this project?
A: I used Google Gemini to generate pregnancy milestone content
   for weeks 8 to 40 in Kannada and English. 33 entries total.
   This content is stored offline in the app.

Q: Why Kannada?
A: The app is for rural Karnataka mothers. Many of them
   don't speak English. Bilingual support makes it accessible.

Q: Does it need internet?
A: No. The app works 100% offline. All data is saved locally.

Q: What are WorkManager workers?
A: They are background tasks that run automatically.
   Like an alarm — even if the app is closed, they still run.

Q: What was your biggest challenge?
A: The Gradle build error where the wrong Java version was picked up.
   I fixed it by pointing Gradle to Android Studio's own JDK
   and stopping the old cached daemon.

Q: What is the minimum Android version your app supports?
A: Android 5.0 (API 21). This covers about 98% of all Android phones.
